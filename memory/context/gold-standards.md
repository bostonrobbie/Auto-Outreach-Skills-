# Gold Standards — reference STRUCTURE per touch

> These are structural calibration references, NOT fill-in templates. They show the *shape* of a strong message so the agent can match the standard. The actual words come from your voice-profile + per-contact research. (This file ships with no real customer copy — your onboarding fills the proof points from `config.json`.)

## Cold email T1 — the 4-paragraph standard
```
Hi {First},

{One diagnostic question grounded in a specific, verified fact about their product or company. Ask, don't assert.}

{One sentence on why that pain compounds for companies like theirs.}

{Customer} used {Product} to {quantified result}, after they stopped {pain}.

{One engagement question inviting them to share their current situation — not a meeting ask.}

{{SIGN_OFF_LINE_1}}
{{SIGN_OFF_LINE_2}}
```
Notes: ~{{MIN_WORDS}}-{{MAX_WORDS}} words · exactly one named proof · two question marks total · no em dashes · no feature headline.

## Follow-up (T2/T3) — shorter, new angle
- Reference the prior touch lightly ("circling back quick").
- Bring a NEW angle or proof, not a rehash.
- Softer, shorter ask. 40-70 words.

## Break-up — shortest (30-50 words)
- Acknowledge the silence without guilt-tripping.
- Never pitch in the break-up.
- Leave the door open: "If the timing isn't right, totally get it — just closing the loop so I'm not clogging your inbox."

## LinkedIn connection request
- One-line intro of who you are + who you connect with, one verified signal line, a low-pressure close.
- No question mark, no em dash, no product name-drops. Short (under ~300 characters).

## Cold-call snippet (3 lines max)
- Line 1: "Hey {Name}, this is {You} from {Company} — {personalized hook}."
- Line 2: "{Specific problem tied to their context}."
- Line 3: "We helped {proof point}. Worth 60 seconds to see if it's relevant?"
- Use a DIFFERENT proof and pain hypothesis than the email/InMail touches.

## What a great message is NOT
> "Hey {Name}, I saw you worked at {Company} for {X} years leading {team}. That must mean {assumption}. {Client} saved {number}. Would 15 minutes make sense?"

That formula is dead. Every prospect has seen it a hundred times. Ask about their world instead of reciting theirs back to them.
