# Final Re-Qualification + Backfill — the last gate before send

Hitting the target number is not the same as sending to the right people. After the compliance audit, the batch is split by confidence. The green (clean, high-confidence) drafts are ready. But every **flagged / medium / low-confidence** draft must be re-qualified with DEEPER research before it's allowed to ship — and any that still can't be confirmed is CUT and REPLACED, so the number holds without dragging weak targets across the line.

This runs AFTER the compliance auditor and BEFORE the pre-send flight check.

## Step 1 — Segment by confidence
- **GREEN:** passed the audit with no flag → ready.
- **YELLOW/RED (flagged):** persona-soft (title passes but the actual role may not own/influence the buying decision), geo edge cases, unverifiable employer/tenure, deliverability risk (e.g. address may be stale post-acquisition), or any agent-raised caveat. These do NOT ship until re-qualified.

## Step 2 — Deep re-qualification of every flagged contact
For each flagged person, spawn a focused verification pass (parallel, one per contact, verified identity handed in) that goes DEEPER than the first look:
- **Sales Nav lead page** (or a full public-profile scroll for About + full Experience + recent activity) — not just the header.
- Resolve the specific flag: does this person actually **own or materially influence software QA / test / release quality**? Is the employer currently confirmed? Is the address deliverable?
- **Strict verdict:** **KEEP** only if the deeper evidence confirms the fit (state the evidence). **CUT** if the deeper look still can't confirm software-delivery/QA relevance, or surfaces a disqualifier (wrong persona, departed, dead address). "Can't tell" = CUT. The bar is confidence, not benefit of the doubt.
- Every KEEP/CUT cites the evidence. No fabrication.

## Step 3 — Cut, with a logged reason
Remove each CUT from the send set. Log name + reason (e.g. "persona: role is data-engineering, not QA/software-delivery; deeper read did not surface test/release ownership"). Add to the do-not-contact list only if it's a hard disqualifier (wrong persona, departed); otherwise just remove from this batch.

## Step 4 — Backfill to hold the number
For every CUT, source a REPLACEMENT so the send count returns to target:
- Source from the same approved universe (prefer a different, less-worked account to avoid over-rotation).
- Run the replacement through the FULL gauntlet: dedup → live research (own tab, verified identity) → company anchor → draft → self-QC → and the SAME compliance audit + this re-qualification gate.
- A replacement that itself comes back flagged is re-qualified too; if it can't confirm, cut it and source another. **Loop until the batch is `target` count of GREEN, re-qualified drafts.**
- Respect same-company distinctiveness (distinct proof/CTA) when the replacement lands at a company already in the batch.

## Step 5 — Certify
The batch is send-ready only when: count = target, every draft is GREEN (audit-passed + re-qualified), zero unresolved flags. Then it proceeds to the pre-send flight check.

## Why this exists
Volume pressure quietly lowers the persona bar — you keep a soft-fit contact "to hit the number." This gate makes the number and the quality bar independent: weak targets get cut, and the count is restored by sourcing *more*, not by shipping the weak ones. The rep should never have to be the one who notices a data-engineer or a departed employee slipped into a "ready" batch.
